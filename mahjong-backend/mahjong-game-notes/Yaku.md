# Mahjong Yaku Reference

## Basic / Situational Yaku

| Yaku | Han (Closed) | Han (Open) | Description |
|------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| **Riichi** | 1 | — | Declare riichi when tenpai with a closed hand, discarding face-down. Closed only. |
| **DaburuRiichi** | 2 | — | Riichi declared on your very first discard of the game. Closed only. |
| **OpenRiichi** | 1 | — | Riichi variant where you reveal your hand. Rare/optional rule. Closed only. |
| **DaburuOpenRiichi** | 2 | — | Open riichi declared on the first turn. Reveals hand. Closed only. |
| **Ippatsu** | 1 | — | Win within one full round of turns after declaring riichi, before any calls interrupt. |
| **Tsumo** | 1 | — | Win by self-draw with a closed hand. Closed only. |
| **Pinfu** | 1 | — | Four sequences, a non-yakuhai pair, and a two-sided (ryanmen) wait. Closed only. |
| **Tanyao** | 1 | 1 | All tiles are simples (2–8); no terminals or honors. |
| **Iipeiko** | 1 | — | Two identical sequences in a closed hand. Closed only. |
| **Ryanpeikou** | 3 | — | Two pairs of identical sequences (double iipeiko). Closed only. |
| **Chiitoitsu** | 2 | — | Seven different pairs. Fixed fu of 25. Closed only. |

## Triplet / Structure Yaku

| Yaku | Han (Closed) | Han (Open) | Description |
|------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| **Toitoi** | 2 | 2 | All four melds are triplets; no sequences. |
| **Sanankou** | 2 | — | Three concealed triplets. The fourth meld may be open. Closed only. |
| **Sanshoku** | 2 | 1 | Three sequences of the same number across all three suits. |
| **SanshokuDoukou** | 2 | 2 | Three triplets of the same number across all three suits. |
| **Ittsu** | 2 | 1 | A 1–2–3, 4–5–6, and 7–8–9 sequence all in the same suit. |
| **Honitsu** | 3 | 2 | Hand uses only one suit plus honor tiles. |
| **Chinitsu** | 6 | 5 | Hand uses only one suit; no honors. |
| **Honroto** | 2 | 2 | All tiles are terminals (1s/9s) or honors, all melds are triplets/pairs. Stacks with Toitoi/Chiitoitsu. |
| **Junchan** | 3 | 2 | Every meld and the pair contain a terminal (1 or 9). |
| **Chantai** | 2 | 1 | Every meld and the pair contain a terminal or honor. |
| **Shosangen** | 2 | 2 | Two triplets of dragon tiles plus a pair of the third dragon. |
| **SanKantsu** | 2 | 2 | Three kans declared in the hand. |

## Special Situational Yaku

| Yaku | Han | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| **Haitei** | 1 | Win by tsumo on the very last drawable tile. |
| **Houtei** | 1 | Win by ron on the discard after the last drawable tile. |
| **Rinshan** | 1 | Win by tsumo on the supplemental tile drawn after declaring a kan. |
| **Chankan** | 1 | Win by robbing a kan — stealing the tile someone adds to an open triplet to complete a kan. |
| **NagashiMangan** | Mangan | All of your discards were terminals or honors, and none were called. Scored as mangan at round end. |
| **Renhou** | Mangan* | Win by ron before your first draw (within the first round of turns). *Scored as mangan or yakuman depending on ruleset. |

## Bonus Han (Dora)

> These are not yaku themselves but add han to a winning hand.

| Bonus | Han | Description |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **Dora** | 1 each | Bonus tile indicated by flipping the dora indicator (next tile in sequence). |
| **AkaDora** | 1 each | Red five tiles (赤ドラ), pre-designated as dora tiles. |
| **UraDora** | 1 each | Revealed only after a riichi win; additional dora indicators flipped from under the dora indicator. |
